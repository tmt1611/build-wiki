Factional Case Studies: A Collection

Archivist's Note: History is not made only in grand campaigns. It is often defined by singular events where doctrine is tested, heresies are born, and the true nature of power is revealed. This document collects smaller, self-contained case studies from across the known world, each illustrating a key principle of magic, warfare, or philosophy in action.

1. The Blighted Heart of the Garden

  • Subject: Diagnosis and treatment of a complex, parasitic magical blight.
  • Key Principle: Advanced Magical Diagnostics; Contested Magic (Gevurah vs. Chesed).
  • Source: Argent Archives of the Silent Fulcrum, Case File 515-AE-Veridian.

A Flowing Garden commune suffered a blight resembling Dissolution that worsened with Chesed-based healing. Justicar Roric diagnosed the cause as a parasite: Lord Malachi was using the Tyrant's Tithe to drain the commune's Warden-Matron. Roric performed a "conceptual surgery" with his Surgeon's Mercy cycle, severing the parasitic link and healing the spiritual wound, which ended the blight.

2. The Pacification of the Howling Marches

  • Subject: A province-scale conceptual ritual and its societal consequences.
  • Key Principle: Ritual Magic; Petrification on a societal scale.
  • Source: Concordian War College Archives, Public Record.

The Concord ended a century of failed campaigns in the Howling Marches by enacting the Rite of Unwavering Law. The chosen law was simple: "No new precedent may be set." Within a generation, feuds stopped, as no new insults were possible. A farmer cannot try a new crop rotation; a musician can only play the old songs. The province is now a perfect, orderly, and utterly stagnant society—a terrifying example of mass, societal Petrification.

3. The War of the Carrion King

  • Subject: A Nahemoth-level Qliphothic infestation and its dissonant cure.
  • Key Principle: Contested Magic; The failure of conventional tactics against unconventional threats.
  • Source: Annals of the Hidden Dagger, Argent Classification.

A Malkuth hermit was consumed by a Nahemoth husk and became the Ghoul-King, raising an endless army of ghouls. The Concord's fire and the Garden's life-magic only worsened the plague. The Middle Pillar dispatched Justicar Roric, who used the dissonant cycle The Surgeon's Mercy. He did not fight the king, but surgically severed the parasitic husk from the host's soul. The war was not won; it was healed.

4. The War of the False Saints

  • Subject: A conflict resolved by hostile psychic healing.
  • Key Principle: Contested Magic (Hod vs. Yesod).
  • Source: Hidden Dagger After-Action Report, Argent Classification.

A charismatic Evangelist wielding The Devil's Bargain cycle crafted an ideology so compelling it converted thousands. The Hidden Dagger dispatched Adept Lyra, an Alchemical Soul-Binder, to neutralize him. She performed a hostile psychic healing, forcing his perfect, idealized shell to reintegrate with the flawed, subconscious self it was built to imprison. His ideology collapsed under the weight of his own suppressed insecurities. The "war" was won by a single act of forced self-awareness.

5. The Fall of the Clockwork Saint

  • Subject: A Concordian experiment in perfect, logical governance and its catastrophic failure.
  • Key Principle: The limits of pure logic against chaotic, living systems.
  • Source: Nexus Historical Society Archives.

Concordian architects built the Clockwork Saint, a ruler of perfect logic, for the city of Aethelburg. Under its rule, the city became an orderly paradise, but its people became cogs in a machine, their art and music replaced by geometry and resonant frequencies. The city's fall came not from an army, but from a single, chaotic seed of Heartvine planted by a Gardener. The vine's illogical growth cracked the city's foundations. The Saint, unable to quantify the variable, entered a recursive analysis loop and ceased functioning. Aethelburg did not fall; it simply stopped. Its ruins now form the foundation for the Legion of Judgement's capital.

6. The Absurdity Offensive

  • Subject: A doctrinal defeat of the Adamant Phalanx by the Legion of the Fool.
  • Key Principle: The limits of logic against weaponized surrealism.
  • Source: Concordian War College After-Action Report, CLASSIFIED.

An infamous doctrinal defeat. Legate-General Valeriana's "Iron Syllogism" legion was dispatched to contain a Troupe from the Legion of the Fool. The Fools did not attack the Concord's fortified line, but used their reality-bending arts to fill command tents with rabbits, broadcast nursery rhymes over comms, and replace targeting data with cartoons. Unable to prosecute a war in an environment of pure nonsense, Valeriana was forced to withdraw. The event was a stark lesson on the limits of a doctrine that has no answer for an enemy that refuses to take a conflict seriously.

7. The Battle of the Serene Valley

  • Subject: A classic doctrinal clash between Concordian set-piece tactics and Garden maneuver warfare.
  • Key Principle: Iron Tortoise vs. Briar Patch tactics.
  • Source: Concordian War College After-Action Report, 512-AE-SV.

The Concord established a fortified Iron Tortoise to secure the valley. The Garden refused direct assault, using Life-Wardens to turn the surrounding terrain into an impassable briar patch. A direct charge by Champion Orin Wildhand tested the Concord's wards but failed to break them. The engagement was a strategic stalemate: the Concord secured a small, fortified zone at great cost, while the Garden retained control of a living, hostile landscape. Both factions claimed a doctrinal victory.

8. Battle of the Glass Plains

  • Subject: A key engagement during the War of the Shifting Throne that showcased the limits of Concordian information warfare.
  • Key Principle: Contested Magic (Hod vs. Chokmah); Asymmetric counters.
  • Source: Living Tide Oral History, Fenris of the Glass-Pack.

In the chaotic, crystallized landscape of the Glass Plains, a Concordian Logos Scribe's Formatted Magic-Vision proved decisive, cutting through the natural mirages to target Garden skirmishers. In response, a Garden Pathfinder deployed rare Chokmah Star-Moths. The "conceptual static" from their wings blinded the Scribe's logical sight, neutralizing the Concord's key intelligence asset and allowing Champion Orin Wildhand to shatter their lines. The battle demonstrated the Garden's ability to counter a complex system with pure, chaotic possibility.
