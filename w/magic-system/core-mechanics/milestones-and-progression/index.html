Pathways of Power: A Guide to Milestones & Progression

This document outlines mage progression through Milestones. Each Milestone marks a new Tier of mastery in a Sefirah, unlocking new abilities and causing permanent changes.

There are five recognized Tiers of mastery for any given Sefirah:

Tier 0: Awakening

The initial, often uncontrolled, opening of a pathway.

Tier 1: Attunement

The first level of conscious control and channeling. This is the minimum requirement for Cross-Path Facilitation (XPY).

Tier 2: Integration

A deeper fusion of the Sefirah's concept with the mage's self.

Tier 3: Dominance

The mage can exert the Sefirah's principles onto their environment and others.

Tier 4: Ascension

A near-perfect embodiment of the Sefirah's concept. This is the highest known level achievable by mortals.

Milestone chart template

The following template is used in each Sefirah's guide. This document is a high-level summary.

Tier Name Requirement Physical Change Metaphysical Change New Abilities Unlocked
T0 Awakening Survive an uncontrolled influx of the Sefirah's energy. A subtle sign (e.g., change in eye color). Aura becomes visible to Magic-Vision. Sporadic, uncontrolled bursts of power.
T1 Attunement Consciously manifest a simple effect three times. The T0 sign becomes permanent and noticeable. Can consciously align with the pathway; sense others with the same attunement. Basic channeling. Enables Cross-Path Facilitation (XPY).
T2 Integration Reshape one's life to align with the Sefirah's concept. A distinct, supernatural trait emerges. Aura develops a unique 'texture' or signature. Intermediate manifestations; passive environmental influence.
T3 Dominance Overcome a powerful external force using the Sefirah's core principle. Body takes on symbolic aspects of the Sefirah (e.g., skin like marble for Binah). Can impose the Sefirah's concept on an area; can peer into the associated Sefirotic Realm. Advanced manifestations; can create semi-permanent constructs.
T4 Ascension Unique and legendary requirements. Transcends biology to become a living embodiment of the Sefirah. Becomes a 'node' on the Tree of Life; can enter their associated Realm to establish a Domain. Conceptual magic; can become a Progenitor and shape their Domain's reality.

Philosophies of Progression: 'Build Tall' vs. 'Build Wide'

While all mages progress through the Tiers, their overall strategy for gaining power generally falls into one of two major philosophies, representing a core doctrinal split between the major factions.

'Build Wide': The Path of the Generalist

  • Philosophy: "Power through versatility." This is the dominant philosophy of the Flowing Garden.
  • Method: The mage focuses on achieving Tier 1 Attunement in multiple, often Dissonant, Sefirot. Their goal is not to achieve supreme mastery of a single concept, but to gain access to the widest possible array of magical "reagents" to fuel complex Cycles.
  • Strengths: Unmatched versatility and adaptability. Wide builds are capable of producing unique, paradoxical effects that are impossible for a specialist. The legendary Alchemical Justicar is the ultimate expression of this path, wielding a 3-node Dissonant cycle that requires attunement to opposing Pillars.
  • Weaknesses: Power is spread thin. A wide-build mage may struggle to overpower a specialist in a direct contest of their respective domains. They are also at higher risk of complex, blended Corruption as they must constantly manage the Dissonance between opposing concepts within their own soul.

'Build Tall': The Path of the Specialist

  • Philosophy: "Power through mastery." This is the foundational philosophy of the Adamant Concord.
  • Method: The mage focuses all their effort on progressing through the Tiers of a single Sefirah, aiming to achieve and even surpass Tier 4 Ascension. They eschew the complexity of Cycles for the overwhelming, absolute authority that comes from embodying a single cosmic principle.
  • Strengths: Absolute dominance within their domain. A tall build achieves power through sheer conceptual density and authority.
  • Weaknesses: Extreme lack of flexibility. A tall-build Binah master may be able to create an unbreakable ward, but they have no tools to heal a wound or inspire a flagging army. Their greatest risk is an extreme, pure form of Corruption; they become a monstrous caricature of their chosen Sefirah, losing all other aspects of their identity.

Parity of Power: Authority vs. Engineering

The power of a "Tall" build specialist and a "Wide" build generalist are considered equivalent at their highest levels, but they achieve this parity through fundamentally different means: Authority and Engineering.

  • The Engineer (Wide Build): A Wide build mage is a magical engineer. They assemble complex effects by combining fundamental principles from different Sefirot into a Cycle. Their power is a function of their knowledge and creativity. To neutralize a fortress, an engineer might devise a complex, three-Sefirot cycle that exploits a structural flaw (Binah), weakens the material (Malkuth), and applies precise force (Gevurah). The result is potent, but requires preparation, knowledge, and managing high Dissonance.

  • The Authority (Tall Build): A Tall build mage is a cosmic principle. Their power is a function of their sheer conceptual weight and focus. A Tier 5 Gevurah master does not need a complex cycle to neutralize the same fortress. By reaching the breaking point, their will is the embodiment of Judgment. They can simply point at the fortress and declare it "flawed," and reality itself is compelled to agree, causing the structure to crumble. They do not engineer a solution; they are the solution. Their power is less versatile but far more direct and absolute within its domain.

This is why the two paths are considered peers. The Engineer can create a near-infinite variety of complex tools to solve problems. The Authority is a single, perfect tool, but one that can solve any problem within its purview with absolute finality.

The Apex of Power: The Breaking Point (Tier 5)

Tier 4 Ascension is the highest level achievable by mortals without fundamentally altering their nature. A Tall build specialist who pushes beyond this point reaches a "breaking point," a state of apotheosis where they cease to be a practitioner of a Sefirah and become a minor avatar of it. This is the source of their tremendous power. A Tier 5 Gevurah master is Judgment; a Tier 5 Chesed master is Life. Their every action is a manifestation of that core principle, granting them a level of authority that is on par with the most complex effects of a Wide build.

The Qliphothic Mirror

This philosophy also applies to the forbidden arts. A Diver who "builds wide" with the Qliphoth becomes a versatile but unstable monster. A Diver who "builds tall," focusing their entire being on communion with a single Husk, is on the direct path to Infestation. Their "breaking point" is the final, irreversible transformation into a true Qliphothic Avatar, the ultimate expression of a single, cancerous ideal.
