Legion Arcanum Summary
The Legion of the Fool Aleph (The Fool) Weaponize surreal chaos to neutralize conflict by making it too absurd to continue.
The Legion of the Magician (The Reality-Scribes) Beth (The Magician) Deconstruct reality by "un-naming" targets, treating the world as a flawed text to be edited.
The Legion of the High Priestess Gimel (The High Priestess) Ends conflict by revealing a "linchpin lie" at its heart, forcing a truce by broadcasting the truth to all combatants.

The Legion of the Lovers (The Vow-Sworn) The Lovers Power comes from unbreakable vows manifesting as arms and armor. Inflexible; self-destructs if vow is broken.
The Legion of the Chariot The Chariot An inexorable, methodical advance that grinds down all obstacles in a straight line toward an objective.
The Legion of Strength Strength "Conceptual Surgery." A paradoxical balance of precise violence (Gevurah) and potent healing (Chesed) is used to "excise" conflict as if it were a disease.
The Legion of the Hermit The Hermit Creates an aura of tranquility so absolute that it makes conflict conceptually impossible, neutralizing an enemy's will to fight.
The Legion of the Wheel of Fortune The Wheel of Fortune Manipulates probability to engineer lucky outcomes for allies and unlikely failures for enemies, treating warfare as a game of chance where they control the deck.
The Legion of Justice Justice Restores equilibrium through precise, karmic "adjustments" instead of direct combat.
The Legion of the Hanged Man The Hanged Man Treats battle as an information economy, willingly sacrificing units to gain a perfect understanding of the enemy's system.
The Legion of Death Death Treats their own lives as a resource to be spent, using tactical self-sacrifice to fuel powerful transformations.
The Legion of Temperance Temperance Neutralizes conflict by healing the underlying psychic trauma that fuels it, rendering armies obsolete.
The Legion of the Devil The Devil Crafts ideologies so beautiful and internally consistent that surrendering to them feels like salvation.
The Legion of the Tower The Tower Identifies and shatters the single point of failure in any system (a fortress, an ideology) with one overwhelming magical assault.
The Legion of the Star The Star Manifests hope as a physical force that can turn a rout into a heroic last stand.
The Legion of the Moon The Moon Rejects individuality for the pack's collective instinct, stalking and overwhelming high-value targets.
The Legion of the Sun The Sun Exposes the foundational lies supporting an enemy's power, causing a catastrophic collapse in morale.
The Legion of Judgement Judgement Imposes a perfect, idealized blueprint onto the physical world, turning chaos into static art.
The Legion of the World The World (Tav) Awakens the dormant spirit of a location (genius loci), turning the environment itself into a sentient ally and weapon.
