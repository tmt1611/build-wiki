Arcanum Legions: A Catalogue of Heresies

While the great factions seek balance, the Arcanum Legions pursue obsession. Each of the 22 heretical legions is a monoculture built around a single Arcanum, taking a magical art to its radical extreme. Their unconventional doctrines can bypass traditional defenses, but their specialization makes them brittle.

Note: A dynamic, searchable version of this catalog is available at the Factions & Figures Catalog Page.

1. The Legion of the Fool

  • Arcanum: Aleph (The Fool)
  • Doctrine: Weaponize surreal chaos to neutralize conflict by making it too absurd to continue.
  • Archetype: The Sacred Fool.
  • Organization: Anarchic. Members form temporary "Troupes" for specific 'pranks,' led by a Master of Revels. Their home is the Murmuring Hills, a surreal region with loose physical laws, and their base is the nomadic "Endless Circus."
  • Strategic Profile: High-mobility disruption. Applies random, non-lethal debuffs and creates a "Chaos Field" where abilities have unpredictable effects.
  • Doctrinal Counters:
    • Adamant Concord: Ineffective. Direct confrontation is useless, as proven when Legate-General Valeriana's famously unflappable "Iron Syllogism" was defeated in the Absurdity Offensive. The Concord's only counter is strategic withdrawal and containment—a de facto victory for the Legion.
    • Flowing Garden: More resilient due to their own chaotic nature. A Netzach Champion can try to find a purpose within the absurdity, turning the prank into a heroic feat.
    • Way of the Middle Pillar: A Hidden Dagger cell is dispatched not to fight the Troupe, but to identify the "Master of Revels" and perform a psychic intervention to restore their sense of purpose.

2. The Legion of the Magician (The Reality-Scribes)

  • Arcanum: Beth (The Magician)
  • Doctrine: Deconstruct reality by "un-naming" targets, treating the world as a flawed text to be edited.
  • Archetype: The Reality-Scribe.
  • Organization: A monastic academy of ascetic scholars led by a "First Scribe." Proctors review all proposed 'edits' to reality to prevent paradoxes. Their fortress-library is the Silent Scriptorium, a place of unsettling order on a remote mesa.
  • Strategic Profile: Elite debuff and sabotage. Applies stacking "deconstruction" effects that strip enemy stats or revert upgraded buildings.
  • Doctrinal Counters:
    • Adamant Concord: Sophist Hunters deploy Logic Bombs to create zones of conceptual static, reinforcing the "source code" of reality and causing the Scribe's un-naming to fail.
    • Flowing Garden: A direct, overwhelming assault led by a Netzach Champion. The Reality-Scribe's art is slow; a flash flood of passion can overwhelm them before the working is complete.
    • Way of the Middle Pillar: The Ghost engages the Scribe on the astral plane, seeking to deconstruct their sense of self—un-naming the un-namer.

3. The Legion of the High Priestess

  • Arcanum: Gimel (The High Priestess)
  • Doctrine: Ends conflict by revealing a "linchpin lie" at its heart, forcing a truce by broadcasting the truth to all combatants.
  • Archetype: The Prophet of the Veil.
  • Organization: A reclusive spiritual conclave structured around receiving and disseminating truth. Ranks include Listeners (data-gatherers), Weavers (analysts), and Speakers (prophets). Their sanctuary is the Echoing Sanctorum in the Great Chime Canyons.
  • Strategic Profile: Alternate-win-condition. Non-combat Seers channel on enemy structures to generate "Revelation," forcing a map-wide truce or victory.
  • Doctrinal Counters:
    • Adamant Concord: Their rigid indoctrination provides a strong defense. A Logos Scribe's formatted vision can filter the "revelation" as hostile data.
    • Flowing Garden: A Chokmah Pathfinder floods the area with a thousand possible truths, drowning out the Priestess's single, absolute revelation in a storm of psychic noise.
    • Way of the Middle Pillar: The Fulcrum manages the outcome, ensuring the truth leads to a balanced resolution, not chaotic collapse.

4. The Legion of the Empress

  • Arcanum: The Empress
  • Doctrine: Forge powerful but dangerously unstable magical "Prototypes" for each battle.
  • Archetype: The Genesis Architect.
  • Organization: A nomadic network of master artisans and apprentices. They establish temporary Unstable Forges where the veil is thin to create their Prototypes. The title of "Architect-Primus" is granted for the most spectacular creation.
  • Strategic Profile: Fields powerful but unstable "Prototype" units that can self-destruct. Can instantly build researched Prototypes at reduced cost.
  • Doctrinal Counters:
    • Adamant Concord: The Iron Tortoise is the ideal counter. Its impenetrable wards withstand the overwhelming force, allowing the unstable prototypes to break against them and self-destruct.
    • Flowing Garden: Guerilla tactics. They refuse to engage the prototypes directly, instead harassing the less-defended Architects and their supply lines, forcing the prototypes to decay uselessly.
    • Way of the Middle Pillar: Surgical strike. A Hidden Dagger cell infiltrates the Empress's forge and sabotages the prototype before it can be deployed.

5. The Legion of the Emperor

  • Arcanum: The Emperor
  • Doctrine: Project a personal vision of a perfect society, creating "Harmony Zones" that pacify resistance.
  • Archetype: The Lawgiver King.
  • Organization: A mobile missionary state ruled by a "Lawgiver-King," with ranks of "Architects of Harmony" (philosophers) and "Arbiters" (field agents). Their home is the city-state of Aethelburg Secundus, a paternalistic paradise.
  • Strategic Profile: Non-violent territory conversion. Wins by expanding "Harmony" influence over the map.
  • Doctrinal Counters:
    • Adamant Concord: An Aegis Legate can Impose a Rule of "Unwavering Duty," making their soldiers conceptually immune to the pacifying effect.
    • Flowing Garden: A Netzach Champion's Aura of Victory directly counters the Harmony Zone, re-igniting the passion for conflict.
    • Way of the Middle Pillar: The Fulcrum can analyze the "Harmony," identify it as an artificial imposition, and broadcast its flaws to those under its influence, breaking the spell.

6. The Legion of the Hierophant

  • Arcanum: The Hierophant
  • Doctrine: "Cure" enemies of flawed ideologies by broadcasting a persuasive truth that converts them.
  • Archetype: The Hierophant Preceptor.
  • Organization: A monastic missionary order led by a "High Preceptor," with ranks of "Deacons" and "Acolytes." Their mobile base is the Cathedral of Resonant Truth, a deployable resonance chamber.
  • Strategic Profile: Non-violent conversion faction. Units have an aura that slowly converts enemies. Ultimate instantly converts a large group.
  • Doctrinal Counters:
    • Adamant Concord: Their soldiers undergo intense psychological conditioning to resist foreign ideologies. Sophist Hunters can deconstruct the Hierophant's "truth" as a flawed logical argument.
    • Flowing Garden: Deploys a Netzach Champion whose own belief is so passionate and charismatic that it acts as a "louder" signal, drowning out the Hierophant's persuasive broadcast.
    • Way of the Middle Pillar: Sends an Alchemical Soul-Binder to "heal" the converted soldiers, restoring their original loyalties by re-integrating their suppressed beliefs.

7. The Legion of the Lovers (The Vow-Sworn)

  • Arcanum: The Lovers
  • Doctrine: Power comes from unbreakable vows manifesting as arms and armor. Inflexible; self-destructs if vow is broken.
  • Archetype: The Vow-Sworn Templar.
  • Organization: A monastic knightly order led by the "First Paragon," with hierarchy based on the profundity of vows. Ranks include the Unsworn (aspirants) and the Oathbound. Their sanctuary is the Adamant Heart, a fortress carved from a geode.
  • Strategic Profile: Hyper-specialized defensive faction. Units gain massive bonuses when assigned a "Vow" that locks their behavior (e.g., "Guard this spot").
  • Doctrinal Counters:
    • Adamant Concord: Does not attack the vow, but the Templar. A Judicator's Trial by Fire is a direct counter to their physical resilience.
    • Flowing Garden: Changes the battlefield. A Life-Warden can grow a dense forest around a Templar vowed to "hold this bridge," making the bridge irrelevant.
    • Way of the Middle Pillar: The Ghost enters the Templar's subconscious to find and heal the psychological root of the vow, causing the power to dissipate without direct confrontation.

8. The Legion of the Chariot

  • Arcanum: The Chariot
  • Doctrine: An inexorable, methodical advance that grinds down all obstacles in a straight line toward an objective.
  • Archetype: The Chariot Engine.
  • Organization: Less an army than a single, unified weapon. Led by an "Engine-Heart" who acts as the spearhead, the main body is "The Phalanx," supported by Unawakened crews. Their territory is "The Furrow" they carve, and their mobile base is "The Procession."
  • Strategic Profile: Slow-moving, high-durability "deathball." Units have a damaging aura that intensifies when grouped. Ultimate makes all units unstoppable.
  • Doctrinal Counters:
    • Adamant Concord: Meets force with superior force. The Iron Tortoise formation is designed to halt exactly this kind of inexorable advance, turning it into a battle of attrition the Concord is built to win.
    • Flowing Garden: Refuses a direct engagement. The Briar Patch tactic turns the ground into a quagmire, separating the Chariot's forces and bogging them down until their momentum is lost.
    • Way of the Middle Pillar: A Hidden Dagger cell identifies the Chariot's single, unwavering objective and removes or neutralizes it before the Legion arrives, rendering their advance pointless.

9. The Legion of Strength

  • Arcanum: Strength
  • Doctrine: "Conceptual Surgery." A paradoxical balance of precise violence (Gevurah) and potent healing (Chesed) is used to "excise" conflict as if it were a disease.
  • Archetype: The Alchemical Justicar. The legion's current leader, the amoral Surgeon-General Kaelen, takes this doctrine to a heretical extreme, seeking to weaponize Corruption itself.
  • Organization: A nomadic, monastic surgical college led by a "Surgeon-General." Ranks include Diagnosticians (intel) and Justicars (field surgeons). They operate from temporary, conceptually sterile field hospitals called Sanatoriums, which are considered neutral ground.
  • Strategic Profile: Elite hero-units that toggle between high single-target damage and powerful area-of-effect healing. Ultimate applies both effects at once.
  • Doctrinal Counters:
    • Adamant Concord: Deploys a team of Sophist Hunters to focus-fire their deconstructive logic on the Justicar, causing their complex Dissonant cycle to collapse.
    • Flowing Garden: Swarms the Justicar with numerous, disposable beasts. Their toggled nature means they cannot be in both offense and defense at once, forcing them into an inefficient state.
    • Way of the Middle Pillar: The ultimate counter is one of their own: a more skilled Alchemical Justicar who can analyze and deconstruct the enemy's cycle.

10. The Legion of the Hermit

  • Arcanum: The Hermit
  • Doctrine: Creates an aura of tranquility so absolute that it makes conflict conceptually impossible, neutralizing an enemy's will to fight.
  • Archetype: The Anchorite of the Wastes.
  • Organization: A contemplative monastic order led by a council of elder "Still-Points." Listeners (scouts) and Anchorites (field agents) use remote, silent places as temporary centers for meditation.
  • Strategic Profile: Non-violent area-denial. Units create mobile "Peace Zones" where all units are disarmed. Ultimate disables all combat on the map.
  • Doctrinal Counters:
    • Adamant Concord: Deploys Unawakened cohorts. The Peace Zone affects the will to fight, which is a magical/psychological phenomenon. Unawakened soldiers, operating on pure discipline and orders, are more resistant.
    • Flowing Garden: A Netzach Champion's passion is so overwhelming it can "burn out" the tranquil aura, re-igniting the will for conflict.
    • Way of the Middle Pillar: They do not counter it, but often hire the Legion of the Hermit to create neutral territory for peace negotiations.

11. The Legion of the Wheel of Fortune

  • Arcanum: The Wheel of Fortune
  • Doctrine: Manipulates probability to engineer lucky outcomes for allies and unlikely failures for enemies, treating warfare as a game of chance where they control the deck.
  • Archetype: The Fortune-Spun Weaver.
  • Organization: A metaphysical casino led by a "Croupier," with ranks of "Weavers," "Pit Bosses," and "Dealers." Their capital is The Calamity Casino, a city in a permanent probability storm.
  • Strategic Profile: RNG-manipulation faction. Auras grant allies high crit/dodge chances and give enemies a chance to miss or have abilities fail. Ultimate guarantees friendly procs and high enemy failure rates.
  • Doctrinal Counters:
    • Adamant Concord: The Concord's doctrine is statistical certainty. They render luck irrelevant by deploying overwhelming numbers and redundant systems.
    • Flowing Garden: Fights fire with fire, deploying their own Fortune-Spun Weavers to create a chaotic "probability storm" where no one can predict the outcome.
    • Way of the Middle Pillar: Deploys a cell whose mission is to protect a single, key asset (e.g., a siege weapon) with a localized aura of stability, ensuring its success while the rest of the battle is left to chaos.

12. The Legion of Justice

  • Arcanum: Justice
  • Doctrine: Restores equilibrium through precise, karmic "adjustments" instead of direct combat.
  • Archetype: The Karmic Arbiter.
  • Organization: A mobile court of law led by an "Arbiter-Primus." Ranks include Auditors (intel) and Adjustors (field agents). Their headquarters is the Hall of Equilibrium, a pocket dimension that manifests when the Legion sits in judgment.
  • Strategic Profile: High-skill faction with powerful single-target "Correction" debuffs (e.g., swap attack/armor values). Ultimate resets a teamfight to its state 10 seconds prior.
  • Doctrinal Counters:
    • Adamant Concord: A Judicator applies a Weight of Judgement debuff that punishes the Arbiter for using their own abilities, creating a feedback loop of self-correction.
    • Flowing Garden: Fields swarms of weaker beasts. The Arbiter's single-target corrections are inefficient against a tide of disposable bodies.
    • Way of the Middle Pillar: The Fulcrum's harmonizing aura can stabilize a "corrected" ally, helping them rapidly return to their normal state.

13. The Legion of the Hanged Man

  • Arcanum: The Hanged Man
  • Doctrine: Treats battle as an information economy, willingly sacrificing units to gain a perfect understanding of the enemy's system.
  • Archetype: The Grand Tactician.
  • Organization: A highly-structured intelligence agency led by a council of "Spymasters." Ranks include Analysts (The Weavers) who process data, and Pawns (The Sacrifices) who act as expendable field agents. Their territory is the Silent Web, a clandestine network of nodes, with a true HQ in the astral Crystal Web.
  • Strategic Profile: Info-warfare and trap-based faction. Disposable units grant vision on death. Ultimate sacrifices their hero for temporary global vision and a massive combat bonus to all remaining units.
  • Doctrinal Counters:
    • Adamant Concord: Deploys Silent Sentinels in "Grey Cloaks" that obscure their aura, rendering them invisible to magical scrying and providing false information.
    • Flowing Garden: A Chokmah Pathfinder's raw, chaotic Magic-Vision can sometimes pierce the Legion's information-gathering techniques.
    • Way of the Middle Pillar: The Hidden Dagger is the ideal counter, being masters of operating without detection and turning the engagement into a battle of spies.

14. The Legion of Death

  • Arcanum: Death
  • Doctrine: Treats their own lives as a resource to be spent, using tactical self-sacrifice to fuel powerful transformations.
  • Archetype: The Phoenix-Born.
  • Organization: A monastic warrior order whose hierarchy is based on the number of rebirths. Led by the "Ashen Council" and the "First Phoenix." Their home is the Ashen Grove, a forest in a constant state of burning and regrowth.
  • Strategic Profile: High-risk attrition faction. Dying units can be resurrected into stronger "Reborn" versions. Ultimate instantly kills all friendly units to damage enemies and resurrect them as elites.
  • Doctrinal Counters:
    • Adamant Concord: A Judicator's Conceptual Severance severs the soul from its magical anchor, preventing resurrection.
    • Flowing Garden: A Life-Warden can use their abilities to pacify and capture enemy units instead of killing them, denying the Legion their core mechanic.
    • Way of the Middle Pillar: Deploys a specialist to pacify the souls of the slain, preventing them from being called back to the fight.

15. The Legion of Temperance

  • Arcanum: Temperance
  • Doctrine: Neutralizes conflict by healing the underlying psychic trauma that fuels it, rendering armies obsolete.
  • Archetype: The Alchemical Soul-Binder.
  • Organization: A monastic healing order led by a "Master Alchemist." Their bases are Stillwater Sanatoriums, hidden refuges of profound peace.
  • Strategic Profile: Non-violent pacification faction. Units "Pacify" enemies over time, converting them to neutral. Ultimate instantly pacifies a target hero and all surrounding units.
  • Doctrinal Counters:
    • Adamant Concord: Deploys soulless automata like Logic-Engines which have no psychic trauma to heal and are thus immune to pacification.
    • Flowing Garden: Counters healing with overwhelming growth, using Life-Wardens to create zones of chaotic life that disrupt the tranquil state required for the Legion's magic.
    • Way of the Middle Pillar: As masters of this art, they deploy their own superior Alchemical Soul-Binders to shield allies and reverse the pacification.

16. The Legion of the Devil

  • Arcanum: The Devil
  • Doctrine: Crafts ideologies so beautiful and internally consistent that surrendering to them feels like salvation.
  • Archetype: The Evangelist.
  • Organization: A messianic cult led by a "Prophet" or "Preceptor." They create Sanctuaries of Belief rather than conquering territory, the most famous being the legendary Resplendent City.
  • Strategic Profile: Psychological warfare faction. Heroes "Preach" to drain enemy "Morale," eventually converting them. Ultimate converts a target hero and all nearby low-morale units.
  • Doctrinal Counters:
    • Adamant Concord: Heavy indoctrination and the use of Unawakened cohorts who are less susceptible to conceptual attacks on belief.
    • Flowing Garden: Overwhelming the beautiful ideology with raw, chaotic, and undeniable emotion from a Netzach Champion.
    • Way of the Middle Pillar: As seen in the War of the False Saints, they dispatch an Alchemical Soul-Binder to perform a hostile psychic healing on the Evangelist, forcing an integration that collapses their perfect, idealized shell.

17. The Legion of the Tower

  • Arcanum: The Tower
  • Doctrine: Identifies and shatters the single point of failure in any system (a fortress, an ideology) with one overwhelming magical assault.
  • Archetype: The Tower-Struck.
  • Organization: An engineering college devoted to studying failure, led by a "Master Analyst." Their laboratory-fortress is the Shattered Spire, a tower in a perpetual state of collapse and reconstruction.
  • Strategic Profile: High-risk, "alpha-strike" faction. "Scans" an enemy target over a long period, then uses their Ultimate to instantly destroy it from anywhere on the map.
  • Doctrinal Counters:
    • Adamant Concord: Redundancy. Their strategy is to have no single, catastrophic failure point by using multiple command centers and rotating key personnel.
    • Flowing Garden: Constant movement and chaos. Their nomadic command structure and lack of a central base make it impossible for the Legion to acquire a stable "target lock."
    • Way of the Middle Pillar: Deploys The Ghost to create a perfect phantasm of a high-value target, tricking the Legion into wasting their ultimate on a decoy.

18. The Legion of the Star

  • Arcanum: The Star
  • Doctrine: Manifests hope as a physical force that can turn a rout into a heroic last stand.
  • Archetype: The Starlight Herald.
  • Organization: A nomadic collective of artists and visionaries led by a "Herald-Primus." They follow celestial alignments, and their central sanctuary is the Well of Aspirations, a hidden spring.
  • Strategic Profile: Pure support faction. Non-combat heroes generate "Inspiration" from nearby fighting, which is spent on powerful buffs. Ultimate makes nearby allies temporarily invulnerable.
  • Doctrinal Counters:
    • Adamant Concord: Targeted elimination. A Judicator is tasked with assassinating the non-combat Herald, neutralizing the source of the buffs.
    • Flowing Garden: A war of attrition. The Garden fields so many units that the Herald cannot inspire them all, overwhelming the buffed units with sheer numbers.
    • Way of the Middle Pillar: The Fulcrum's harmonizing aura can subtly dampen the inspiring effect, reducing its potency without a direct confrontation.

19. The Legion of the Moon

  • Arcanum: The Moon
  • Doctrine: Rejects individuality for the pack's collective instinct, stalking and overwhelming high-value targets.
  • Archetype: The Primal Shepherd.
  • Organization: A feral, nomadic pack whose hierarchy is based on strength, led by an "Alpha" and a "Huntmaster" (strategist). They claim vast wildernesses as hunting grounds.
  • Strategic Profile: Aggressive, high-mobility "swarm" faction of beast-units buffed by non-combat Shepherd heroes. Ultimate triggers a "Frenzy" state, making beasts uncontrollable but massively increasing their speed and damage.
  • Doctrinal Counters:
    • Adamant Concord: Area denial. Aegis Legates create conceptual wards like "The Ground is Quicksand" to immobilize the swarm before it can close the distance.
    • Flowing Garden: The Garden's own Beast-Callers can attempt to challenge the Shepherd for dominance over the pack's alpha instincts.
    • Way of the Middle Pillar: A Hidden Dagger cell assassinates the Shepherd. Without their guidance, the frenzied pack becomes a chaotic force that attacks anything, including its own allies.

20. The Legion of the Sun

  • Arcanum: The Sun
  • Doctrine: Exposes the foundational lies supporting an enemy's power, causing a catastrophic collapse in morale.
  • Archetype: The Luminary Inquisitor.
  • Organization: A hierarchical, inquisitorial order led by an "Inquisitor-Solar." Their fortress is the Helian Observatory, a tower of white stone and mirrors on a high mountain peak.
  • Strategic Profile: Psychological warfare faction. Units apply "Revelation" debuffs that cause enemies to attack each other or flee. Ultimate reveals the map and causes all enemies to treat everyone as hostile.
  • Doctrinal Counters:
    • Adamant Concord: Tactical withdrawal. The Concord's disciplined forces are trained to fall back to a fortified position and regroup the moment their cohesion is compromised by a conceptual attack.
    • Flowing Garden: Embraces the chaos. Their forces are already a loose collection of individualistic warriors; a free-for-all is a state in which they are comfortable fighting.
    • Way of the Middle Pillar: The Ghost can create a counter-illusion of a "true" enemy, redirecting the induced paranoia and chaos towards a single, phantom target.

21. The Legion of Judgement

  • Arcanum: Judgement
  • Doctrine: Imposes a perfect, idealized blueprint onto the physical world, turning chaos into static art.
  • Archetype: The Master Artificer.
  • Organization: A hierarchical artisan's guild led by an "Artificer-Primus." Their home is New Aethelburg, a city of flawless crystal built on the ruins of the failed Clockwork Saint experiment.
  • Strategic Profile: Elite "deathball" faction. Attacks have a chance to "Petrify" enemies, turning them into inert statues. Ultimate mass-petrifies all enemies in a large area, permanently removing them.
  • Doctrinal Counters:
    • Adamant Concord: Superior petrification. Their own masters of Binah (Aegis Legates) and Gevurah (Judicators) are more skilled in the art of binding and control. It becomes a duel of specialists.
    • Flowing Garden: A Chesed Life-Warden can reverse the petrification effect, albeit slowly, by infusing the statues with a wave of life-giving energy.
    • Way of the Middle Pillar: Deploys a single Unawakened operative with a mundane sledgehammer. Petrification makes targets hard but also brittle—a simple, physical solution to a magical problem.

22. The Legion of the World

  • Arcanum: The World (Tav)
  • Doctrine: Awakens the dormant spirit of a location (genius loci), turning the environment itself into a sentient ally and weapon.
  • Archetype: The Genius Loci.
  • Organization: A decentralized network of genius loci led by the "Parliament of Loci." Ranks include Wanderers (apprentices) and Wardens (masters of a territory). Their "army" is the Awakened Land itself.
  • Strategic Profile: Extreme territorial defense. Nearly unbeatable on home ground, which they can animate and control. Units are spawned directly from the environment.
  • Doctrinal Counters:
    • Adamant Concord: Scorched earth policy. A Judicator is dispatched not to fight the awakened land, but to systematically destroy it with Trial by Fire, rendering it conceptually barren.
    • Flowing Garden: Fights territory with territory. A powerful Life-Warden can use their own abilities to "re-wild" the Warden's domain, contesting their control in a battle of two different dreams for the same piece of land.
    • Way of the Middle Pillar: Surgical strike. The Ghost slips through the awakened land's defenses to neutralize the Warden themselves. With the central consciousness gone, the awakened land falls dormant.
